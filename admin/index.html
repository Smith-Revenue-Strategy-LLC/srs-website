FAQ and Common Objections

Frequently Asked Questions

Who's a good fit for a Smith Revenue Strategy?

Consultants. Contractors. Privately held businesses with revenues ranging from $500,000 to $100 M.

Our business is not structured enough/too messy to deploy custom software or agentic automation technology

Matt: building out automations, even if you know of 1 repeatable task starts laying down the foundations to start automating and building structured procedures for other areas in the business.

When would my company be a right fit for automated workflows?

Matthew: you tell me! If you don't want to automate repeatable tasks then you don't have to. Think of automation and AI assisted workforce as a way to supplement your current workforce - that way they can focus on what's really important and not the mundane.

Our business has too many nuances and how we operate to be able to use an automated technology system.

Matthew: AI thrives in the nuance as it has near unlimited imagination as long as the driver has the vision. Therefore, if you have the vision, AI can be the excellent partner in bringing it out into reality.

The large language models simply aren't consistent enough and we can't afford to have hallucinations run our core business functions.

Matthew: Predictions and Machine Learning can be inaccurate but that is generally avoidable during a curation phase. Humans have been known to be inaccurate as well. The benefit of the LLM is that you can be curating an unlimited number of ML models and then seeing which one is most accurate for your intended purpose.

I don't want to send my business data off to the cloud or one of the model makers (like OpenAI/Anthopic/Google, etc.) to make money off of my business intel.

Matthew: There are LLM solutions that are capable of being internal only. Not connecting to the internet. Along with a custom build application with LLM can all be internal. Generally, the cloud models trade off the infrastructure cost for subscription. The Cloud providers and model makers do offer private/non-used tenant spaces that allows your data to not be used.

I have concerns about the security risks of having agentic automation solutions or running large language models (LLM's) on my own server/hardware/network.

Matthew: This is no different than current software solutions or current operating systems. There needs to be a security management strategy already in place so that when an LLM/AI is used then the same principles would be applied - see NIST CSF 2.0.

My industry is heavily regulated and I have to follow too many compliance requirements to be able to use AI solutions in my business.

Matthew: Most providers have compliance compliant tenant environments. For the LLM, hosting it local is also a capability, however, this does drive up infrastructure cost as mentioned earlier but you have full and total control of your data.

The software we use is very old and other vendors we've talked to have struggled to integrate with it or the integration expense was not justified by the outcomes we were expecting to come from it.

Matthew: This is not a new problem. The build vs buy (rent) has always been there. Now, the shift to building is more obtainable. Rather than having to use a solution that inhibits revenue growth, then investing in a solution that is custom tailored to your environment provides more business and process control that influences efficient operations.

I can't justify the cost of building a custom solution because I see this technology becoming out of date every six months or so... I need a model agnostic solution for my business to remain relevant

Matthew: Integration changes are rapidly evolving. We now have MCP along with APIs, which is a massive shift in how the industry will communicate. However, that doesn't mean everyone is going to adopt this new technology right from the start. Successful businesses are successful due to the processes that the people are successfully implementing. Technology is just a tool to assist in that process. Technology needs to be planned in a way that if there is another evolution then the product needs to be able to adapt to the changes quickly.

I don't have the time to stop and fine tune agentic automation to our unique business parameters and vertical specific nuances.

Matthew: Managed Service Providers and Support contracts is not a new concept and it should be looked at in a way of what's best for your company. Some companies have the personnel to fine tune agentic automations while others are solo shops. The solution delivered needs to be built in a way where you don't need daily fine tunings.

I don't have highly technically inclined people on my team who can manage these tools if you were to set them up for us and leave them in our care.

Half our team can barely remember their password... I don't expect them to use any AI driven technologies day-to-today. (Change Management Consulting and ongoing support services)

We already have ChatGPT and Microsoft Copilot and don't use either one to their full capacity. Why should we expect to get more benefit from working with an AI consulting group now?

Matthew: ChatGPT and Microsoft's Copilot are in an entirely different category for what is currently available in the market. Those are seen in the automation space as helpful LLMs to chat with and maybe look some things up. However, most of the tools that actually do the automated tasks, collaborate data, unify systems - are actually other tools that are hot swappable, but are driven by coding assistant solutions.
