Engagement path

What happens first?

The process is designed to keep the first decision small, practical, and evidence-based before any larger implementation recommendation is made.

01

Complimentary Connect Call

We start with a 30-minute initial consult to understand your situation and whether SRS can help.

02

Workflow Assessment

If the problem is ready, we recommend the right assessment option based on your project scope.

03

Findings and Decision Path

We review what can be automated, eliminated, or simplified and summarize the clearest path forward.

Assessment options

Choose the assessment that matches the problem.

Workflow Assessment

Focused problem. Clear owner.

Includes

  • Two 45-minute sessions
  • Leading executive
  • Stakeholder in project business unit

Best for

Smaller scope or earlier-stage operations where the friction is already fairly well defined.

Full Workflow Analysis

Complex workflow. Full walk-through.

Includes

  • Three 1-hour sessions
  • Leading executive and project stakeholder
  • Full walk-through with front-line team

Best for

Complex, multi-department operations or cross-system workflows where thoroughness matters.

Decision criteria

A good recommendation should make the tradeoffs visible.

Before implementation, SRS helps leaders weigh business value, team adoption, security requirements, data readiness, and ongoing support needs.

Business value What changes if the workflow improves, and how that impact should be tracked.
Technical reality Whether existing systems can support the process or need a different operating layer.
Human adoption How the workflow will be documented, governed, and used by the team after launch.
